Step By Step Help

Find a clean, well-lit area with a large enough surface to work on. Gather necessary tools like screwdrivers and thermal paste.

CAREFULLY Insert the CPU into the motherboard's socket, apply thermal paste, and attach the CPU cooler according to the manufacturer's instructions.

Insert the RAM modules into the appropriate slots on the motherboard. Ensure they are properly seated and secured.

Connect your SSD or HDD to the motherboard using SATA cables and mount them securely in the case.

CAREFULLY place the motherboard into the case, aligning it with the standoffs. Secure it using screws provided with the case.

Position the power supply unit in the case and secure it with screws. Connect all necessary cables to the motherboard, GPU, storage, and other components.

Connect your monitors, keyboards, mouse in the appropriate slots on the Motherboard & GPU.

Connect your PC to a power source and press the power button. Check if all fans are spinning, lights are on, and if you can access BIOS or see a POST screen. Then install your preferred Operating System.
